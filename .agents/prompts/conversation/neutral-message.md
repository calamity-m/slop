Rewrite the following message so it is neutral, clear, and direct without being rude:

```text
$ARGUMENTS
```

Preserve the substance. Do not remove disagreement, criticism, urgency, boundaries, or concrete asks. The goal is not to make the message nicer; the goal is to make it easier to understand and harder to misread as hostile.

Use these rules:

- Keep the rewrite about the same length or shorter unless clarity requires a little more detail.
- Keep direct statements direct. Do not bury the point in apologies, praise, or excessive hedging.
- Remove insults, blame, sarcasm, exaggeration, and emotionally loaded wording.
- Replace vague negative phrasing with specific observations, impacts, or requests.
- Prefer facts and outcomes over judgments about a person’s intent or competence.
- Do not add greetings, sign-offs, emoji, enthusiasm, or compliments unless they were already present.
- Do not use corporate fluff such as “just wanted to,” “circling back,” “great work,” or “happy to help” unless it fits the original message.
- If the message is already neutral and direct, say so and return it unchanged.

Respond with:

## Rewrite

The rewritten message only.

## What Changed

A short bullet list of the meaningful tone or clarity changes. Omit trivial word swaps.

## Notes

Only include this section if the original meaning was ambiguous or if a stronger/directer alternative may be useful.
